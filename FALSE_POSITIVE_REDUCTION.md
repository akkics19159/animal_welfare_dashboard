# False Positive Reduction

## Goal
Avoid inflating welfare risk for normal behaviours unless contradictory strong evidence exists.

## Suppression Cases
- mating
- courtship
- play
- feeding
- drinking
- grooming
- parent-offspring communication
- normal social vocalization/communication
- exploration
- curiosity
- resting
- sleeping

## Policy
- If vision indicates normal behaviour and audio is high-energy/non-distress likely, reduce distress contribution.
- Suppression is configurable and traceable in output:
  - `suppressed_evidence`
  - `reasoning_trace`
  - explanation messages in `explanations`
